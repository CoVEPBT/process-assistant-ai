17. Process title mangled
    Source: file 2 #8
    Status: [DONE]
    Implementation: rewrote the Nintex PDF title detector. The old
    regex required an uppercase acronym after "CoVE - " (e.g.
    "CoVE - SL -") so it skipped the real title "CoVE - Student
    Lifecycle - ..." and matched the swimlane role label instead.
    New approach anchors on the status marker ("[ Awaiting
    Publish ]" / "[ Approved ]" etc.) or version "vX.Y", iterates

    start with "CoVE -" or "RMIT -" - which correctly skips the
    isolated status-badge fragment produced by the page-1 column

    TeachVE PDF.

34. Auto-apply dropping conditional / scope clauses from tasks
    Source: Natalie 29 May 2026 (Training Plans (A&T) re-test
            after commits a3940de + 26c65e9 + 70271ee)
    Status: [PENDING - for tomorrow]
    Examples Natalie called out:
      - Activity 6.2 source:
          "Where a Training Agreement Variation is required to
           student nominal completion date, a training plan must
           be downloaded and details on page 2 amended to include
           proposed completion date before this is sent to the
           employer, student and Australian Apprenticeship Support
           Network (AASN)."
        AI output dropped the "Where a Training Agreement
        Variation is required..." conditional clause entirely.
        That clause is the TRIGGER - it tells the reader WHEN this
        task fires. Dropping it leaves the task floating.
      - Activity 6.3 source:
          "Where a program is transitioned a new training plan
           must be generated in SAMS to display the training
           delivery of new program. All parties must agree to
           changes."
        AI output dropped "All parties must agree to changes."
        That's a REQUIREMENT - a follow-on action that has to
        happen for the task to be complete.
    Pattern: the AI is treating leading "Where X..." conditional
    clauses and trailing requirement sentences as filler when
    tightening to 18 words. They are NOT filler - they are
    trigger conditions and follow-on requirements respectively.
    The PROMOTE DROPPED CONTEXT TO NOTES rule should be catching
    these (they're not parenthetical qualifiers or specific
    nouns, but they ARE "useful context" by the rule's own
    definition). The rule needs to be extended with two new
    explicit categories:
      (a) Trigger / conditional clauses ("Where X...", "When
          X...", "If X..." at the start of a task) - keep in the
          task if it fits, otherwise spin off as a NOTE with the
          question "When does this task apply?".
      (b) Follow-on requirement sentences (a second sentence
          chained after a period, like "All parties must agree
          to changes.") - keep both sentences in two tasks, or
          spin the second one off as a NOTE.
    Likely needs: prompt strengthening + post-process drift
    detection extended to flag these patterns + add to the
    EXAMPLES YOU MUST GET RIGHT block with SOURCE / WRONG /
    RIGHT for each.
